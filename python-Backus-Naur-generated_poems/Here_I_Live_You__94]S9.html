Here I Live You


Of your red lake when you hold out your mouth.
What we say drinks to
breathe some other mountaineer
what a metaphor may teach.
You are the chaotic cousin of a bird,
the hollow ness of the land, the power of the lava.
The custodian smiles at the lady
but the pioneer does not smile
when he looks at the pheasant lady
and the fatherless ocean.
