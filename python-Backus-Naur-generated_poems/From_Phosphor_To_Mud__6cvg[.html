From Phosphor To Mud


In my field at sunrise you are like a kis
and your form and colour the way I crystallize them.
In your leg of sobbing the vicinity begins to dream of rescuing.

Our new illusion of painters, our ancient home quadrangles.
For a day, maybe twenty-seven,
I rested under a unkindness of ravens
at a bazzar, waiting for the daughter to be in
front of.
