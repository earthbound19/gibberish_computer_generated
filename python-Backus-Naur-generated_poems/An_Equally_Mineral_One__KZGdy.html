An Equally Mineral One


To seek another land a peal of bells protecting will love
the violenet rock of a planet.
Around the flower of the modern office where you sleep,
a dream disguises into inscriptions.

A electrical mist of bevies of ladies.
And you rise like a fold of chairs and flag of a fainted bitten root.
For ripple was lewd and morally negative.
Around the field I like to blush like a directionless goblet.


Dawning toward the angel I was without doubt the pioneer pheasant
there in the bruised universe.
When it looked me with its vertical apple eyes
it had neither shoulder nor heart
but bolt of fused quartz foams on its sides.
