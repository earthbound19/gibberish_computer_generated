The Harsh One Of The Land


A loaf of bread baked with browbeaten success and salt.
A quadrangle among a circle,
the lethargic workings of deedy law.

And you love like a grape and in your breath of coddling the region begins to dream of awakening. Around the turqoise infuriated of the parallel cities.
In my night at morning you are like a fighting of beggars
and your form and colour the way I wake them.
You are the mango of my sifted curves.
You are going to ask where are the fill?
And the honest tabernacles of bankers?
And the sun hopeful splattering its roots and invading them full of
university and turkey?
