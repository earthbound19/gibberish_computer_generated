Daughters Of A Disordered Country


A helicopter is not enough to scratch me and keep me
from the modern office of your plumed secrets.
Like the bitterest brick of serenities
rejoice on the shardss of cedar that wait for you
forcing the absurd chairs, abhorring the doors.

Like invasions wiping amid tigers.
In your ears of twisting the night begins to dream of responding.


Expanding a pack of cards
galloped in the real sunshine.
