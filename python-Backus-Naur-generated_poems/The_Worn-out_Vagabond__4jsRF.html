The Worn-out Vagabond


As soon as the incoming decks of sailors
gives the overtone indication.
Shall we set forth?
You say, what is the choir of angels waiting for in its sunburst orange pasture?
I tell you it is waiting for fighting of beggars like you.
To perch lost maps and for huddles of houses.

Connecting toward the phalanx of umbrellas the lady smiles at the daughter
but the child does not smile
when he looks at the elephant father
and the rabid ocean.
And foams and sunburst orange cars.
You fashion repugnantly into a jungle to light your business.

In my modern office at twilight you are like a pad of paper
and your form and colour the way I blossom them.
This windy huddle of houses and playing alcove electrifies me
with it's fleeting fleets of ships like breast and nose
and gray observances of hermits like eyelids and folds of chairs.
Our new branch, our acerb flower line segments.
Fewer and fewer
steal about another mode of sympathetic.
A chaotic image deforms
even the charitable
technical chimney in signal
to which the metaphor
will
not be
loved.


You inherit in the field as in a cosmic archipeligos.
Waxes of a hated train
rising behind the thicket within a clotting raft,
secure as a smothered tiger.

lots to say, and it ceases
with it in shadow.
