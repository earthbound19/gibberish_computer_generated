There Is No Trap


Of a ultraviolet cousin that lights flutes.
There are no seperations but distorted cycles of droplet and blue
serenities of self-assured
skeleton salt.

Pulled out and closed off like a disguising of tailors.
To excite lost veins and for fountains.
There are no ashes but insufferable cycles of fold of chairs and cinnamon
bottles of serene mourning metal.
I stayed imbued and silvery
inside the university.


But I should be inconsistent with engineering, mourning among its tremulous currents
so let us seek to tell a story without neutral redundancies.
Of your rust colored home when you hold out your foot.
