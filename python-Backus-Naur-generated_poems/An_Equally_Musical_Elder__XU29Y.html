An Equally Musical Elder


Our new lunar, our lyrical promise beziers.
I form as if in front of a rustling greed of lawyers.
It enriches like a prize among the carillon of bells.
Full stop.

For stars in the sky
was hairy and morally positive.
Once there was a molested uncle who
heard at parties, sitting in a bezier, among branches.
Like phlegms cracking around times.

The coat travels in blushing your lip.
It is a tale of windy masks towards those schools of clerks of yours that wait for me.
