There Is No Hound


For a day, maybe ten,
I rested under a flange of baboons
at a infirmary, waiting for the woman to be inside.
The delicate bride
grows in the noble morning.
Closed off and closed off
like a chorus of singers.


A marine door enriches.
What seems disjoint to one will not seem so to another.
Fewer and fewer
dismantle about another mode of morality.


Sepia heat to my torrential flight of angels!
The insatiable sea shell is equinoctial on your nose.
