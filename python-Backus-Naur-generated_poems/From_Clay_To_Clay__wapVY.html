From Clay To Clay


For a day, maybe quintillion,
I rested under a business of ferrets
at a power plant, waiting for the god to be next to.
Shady weather, sifted lights like the goring of butchers.

Some entertain but I kiss your clay like drunkship of cobblers.

In my field at late afternoon you are like a wing of aircraft
and your form and colour the way I rejoice them.
My heart moves from being arrogant to being fleeting.
This insufferable flight of angels and seizing saxophone abolishes me
with it's domestic telegraphs like foot and eyeballs
and brimstone sweetness like finger and thoughts of barons.

Indicates the giggle of girls's blossoming eyelids.
