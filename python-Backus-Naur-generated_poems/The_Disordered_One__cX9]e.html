The Disordered One


You seek unscrupulously into a heights to excite your business.
Enjoy the many lonely attempts to weave
the unguessed city.
There is mineral fortune in mixing it.


Mingle on the cold fires that wait for you
fainting the dead chairs, decaying the doors.
Only silent and to a
gentleman they
take on time, quadrillion years


And you relax like a slate of candidates and what we say
fashions to
reflect some other cousin
what a synonym may teach.
A alcove forming will flow
the dead silica of a planet.
