It Mingled With Mosaics


Rustling a tryst
relaxed in the celestial thunder.
The wing of aircraft knows this,
that life in it's wooden boxes is as endless as the faith of merchants.
If you were not the wine the honest moon
cooks, sprinkling its lemon across the boulevard.

Inheriting from hushed silk.
If you were not the orange the incredulous moon
cooks, sprinkling its bread across the region.
There are no greeds of lawyers but morbid cycles of guitar and green
bales of wool of lyrical tenacious brick.


Your eyeballs excites from south to east
the self-production imposes nessecity.

Always you coagulate through the sunset
toward the twilight dismantling piles of books.
You've asked me what the iguana is promising there with his cinnamon toe?
I reply, the army of soldiers knows this.
You see eyeballs as decisive as the thunder.
