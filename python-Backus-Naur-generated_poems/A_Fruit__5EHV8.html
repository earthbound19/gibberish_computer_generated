A Fruit


The I in crown I was without doubt the mountaineer iguana
there in the wet-winged land.
When it looked me with its moonlit change of bells eyes
it had neither brain nor eye
but gem fightings of beggars on its sides.
To appreciate
lost bells and for pinheads of angels.
Full stop.
I salute your absorbant plum
and envy your ancient pride.
